 Focal Point    Elements of Programming - Program Readability
 Learning   Repetition is a way of life.  We do things over and over.  Every day we get up, eat, brush our teeth, etc.  Day in and day out we repeat.

Computer programs are often dependent on repetition.  A program to print out every alphabet character, using what we now know, would need 52 print statements to print out the upper and lower case alphabet!  Wouldn't it be nice to shorten that to three lines of code?

Repetition in a computer program is done through a Loop

There are basically three different types of loops with which we will be dealing

Do While

  • Condition is analyzed before any processing is done
  • Processing may not be done at all depending on the condition
  • You may have to process sometimes before the loop

Do Until

  • Processing is always done once
  • This is because the condition is analyzed at the end of the loop

For Next

  • You may use the mechanism for counted loops in the book
  • I do prefer that you use the For Loop

Key similarities/differences:

The Do While and the Do Until structures are similar in that a group of statements are repeated in accordance with a specified condition.

The Do While and the Repeat Until structures are different in that a Do While structure tests the condition at the beginning of the loop and a Repeat Until structure tests the condition at the end of the loop.  This means that the statements will be executed in a RepeatUntil loop once before the condition is even tested.

The For Next loop is different from the previous loops in that its counter is incremented automatically.  A For Next loop is used only when a determinable number of repetitions is required.

* * *WARNING * * *

Be careful to avoid infinite loops, that is, loops that are never exited.
