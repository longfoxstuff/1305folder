 Focal Point    Planning Your Program - Flowcharts for Decisions
 Learning    Flowcharts describe how processes work or how decisions are made.

 In Flowcharts, the following symbols are often used:

 Terminal:  Beginning or end of program or process

Process:  A single process in a program or algorithm such as assigning a value, performing a calculation, reading input, or writing output.

Predefined Process:  A named process containing a series of program steps specified elsewhere.  A predefined process has its own flowchart

Decision:  A condition that determines a specified path to follow based on a comparison of two values or the state of a Boolean value

Flowlines:  Lines connecting various symbols in a flowchart.  They contain an arrowhead only when the flow of control is not from top to bottom or left to right.

Input/Output:  A place to get data (from the user or a file) or display information

Connector:  Entry from or exit to another part of the flowchart on the same page

Connector:  Entry from or exit to another part of the flowchart on another page

 IBM Flowchart Template

Flowcharts are commonly used in business to show the flow of a process from its inception to its completion. They usually consist of boxes connected by lines, arranged in such a way as to lead you through a series of steps in the correct order, one box at a time. Sometimes the process a box represents will be stated right in the box, if there is room. If not, then symbols are used to refer to text somewhere else that describes what the box represents. Overall, a flowchart allows one to get a handle on the entire process, while keeping various aspects of that process in perspective, such as their order and how they relate to each other.

Almost anything with a logical flow can be flowcharted.  Here are some examples:

  • Registration for the Singapore Education System
  • The Dark Matter Flowchart - A silly flowchart by smart people
  • The Revelation of John

As you can see there are many different styles of flowcharts and they can be used to describe almost every type of decision making process or logical sequence of events.

In Class Group Work:  Working with one or two others draw out on paper a flowchart describing the waking up process.  Don't forget to include things like showering, brushing your teeth, checking the time, checking the calendar to see what day it is, and eating breakfast if you have time (it is the most important meal of the day!).  Include some decisions like what to do if you wake up late, what if you have no milk in the fridge but you're hungry, what to do if your mom, dad, spouse, other is in a bad mood and yells at you, etc.  Have fun with this for a few minutes.

In Class Group Work:  Using Microsoft Word Art draw a flowchart describing the process of ordering food in our cafeteria.  Remember, you have choices over there, and; don't forget to pay!

 
 Assignment   Individually:  Create a algorithm to describe what you do from the time you leave your house until you are in your vehicle and are on the road. Be prepared to create a flowchart in class for this assignment.  Don't forget that you might unlock your doors manually or you may use a button on a key fob.  You can have problems finding your keys.  You may have a car parked behind you.
